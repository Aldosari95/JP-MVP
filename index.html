Overview of Al Zubarah

Al-Zubarah, located on the northwest coast of the Qatari peninsula, flourished as a trading and fishing hub during the late 18th and early 19th centuries. AlZubarah's economic prosperity, related to the export of pearls and commodity trade, gave rise to many diverse settlements.

Settlement of Zubarah

In the beginning of the 18th century, Al-Zubarah emerged as a vital economic settlement on the West coast of the Arabian Gulf, especially after the invasion of the Persians in the year 1775/76. Its prominence attracted many tribal confederations such as the Utba (pl: Utoob) of from al-Hadar, who settled for a short period of time before returning to Kuwait.

Economy of Zubarah

The location of Al Zubarah played an important role in its economic boom. Located near a vast number of pearl banks, Al Zubarah was an eminent trading hub known for being the center of pearl diving and trade in the Gulf region trading with India and many other countries. Al Zubarah was also an important transit destination for commerce between the West and the East during the 18th century and up until the 19th century.
